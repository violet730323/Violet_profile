Built a two-path recommendation engine to surface cross-sell opportunities across 1.13 million B2B customer accounts. A core finding was that 66% of registered accounts had never placed an order in two years — a segment that had not been previously analyzed and became the basis for a dedicated cold-start strategy.

01 / 05 Problem Statement

When reps rely on memory alone

Sales representatives were managing 386,000 active accounts with no systematic tool to guide cross-sell conversations. Opportunities were missed not because the data was unavailable, but because no infrastructure existed to act on it.

Core challenge

Every rep-customer interaction was reactive. Reps quoted only what was requested. No tool existed to proactively surface relevant products the customer had not yet considered.

What we set out to build

A recommendation engine that generates two ranked product lists per customer: one based on what similar customers buy, and one based on what complements what the customer already owns.

Why two paths are necessary

A model trained on purchase history fails entirely for customers with no purchase records. 745K accounts had zero transactions. A separate cold-start strategy was required specifically for them.

Scale

Three source datasets, over 115 million transaction rows, 1.13 million registered accounts. All processed in Python using DuckDB to avoid memory overflow on standard hardware.

Explore each path in detail

PATH ONE: PERSONALIZED RECOMMENDATIONS

Active customers are assigned to one of 14 behavioral clusters. Two ranked lists are generated per customer using a dual-engine approach.

HYBRID LIST
Find the 10 most similar customers within the cluster using cosine similarity. Pool their purchase histories and blend with cluster-level popularity signal.
COMPLEMENT LIST
For each item the customer already owns, identify products frequently bought alongside it by other customers in the cluster. Filter to exclude same-subcategory items to avoid substitutes.
HYBRID SCORE (0.65 × purchase_signal + 0.35 × popularity_signal) × brand_penalty
COMPLEMENT SCORE (0.70 × max_linkage) + (0.30 × avg_linkage)
brand_penalty: private label products × 1.0 / third-party products × 0.65
PATH TWO: COLD START BRIDGE GROUP STRATEGY

No purchase history means collaborative filtering fails entirely. The solution: match dormant accounts to the most behaviorally similar active-customer segment, then recommend what that segment actually buys.

01
IDENTIFY
Active customers grouped into 47 sub-segments using institutional taxonomy
02
MATCH
Each dormant account matched to the sub-segment sharing the same institution type
03
RECOMMEND
Top products ranked by distinct customer penetration. Fallback to class level if no exact match
Result: every cold-start customer receives a ranked product list matched to their institution type, ready for sales rep outreach

04 / 05 Key Findings

What the data revealed

FINDING_01
The invisible majority
745K accounts
Of 1.13 million registered accounts, 745,000 — a 66% dormancy rate — had placed no orders in two years. Tenure data confirmed these were long-standing accounts, not new sign-ups, that never converted.
These accounts have already cleared the acquisition barrier — activation through targeted recommendations is a lower-cost path to revenue than new customer outreach.
FINDING_02
SKU concentration
91.2% of volume
The top 10% of SKUs accounted for 91.2% of all purchases; 46% of purchased SKUs were bought by fewer than five customers. Most accounts repeatedly ordered the same core items, with little exposure to the broader catalog.
This reflects a lack of visibility, not a lack of need — customers buy what they're shown. The recommendation system surfaces relevant products outside each customer's existing purchase history.
FINDING_03
Private label opportunity
31% mean share
Across 386,103 active customers, mean private-label share was ~31% — nearly 70% of purchases flowed to third-party suppliers. Since private-label carries higher margins, this gap is a measurable, recoverable profit opportunity.
High-spending accounts with low private-label share are the highest-priority targets — their volume is already proven, so a brand-mix shift has outsized impact. A brand uplift layer redirects eligible purchases toward McKesson private-label alternatives.
FINDING_04
Segment-level dormancy
75% in LTC
Long Term Care shows the highest dormancy rate at 75%. Primary Care carries the largest volume of dormant accounts at 423,585. Post Acute Care shows the widest gap between its active and dormant sub-segments.
All three segments share the same pattern: real, ongoing demand that never converted. These accounts represent acquisition costs already spent but revenue not yet realized — the most actionable targets for cold-start recommendations.
