01 / 05 Problem Statement

Why patients leave — and when it's too late to act

Lackey Clinic serves patients at 139–300% of the federal poverty line. Losing patients doesn't just affect care continuity — it directly impacts the clinic's ability to secure funding and sustain operations.

Core challenge

The clinic had no way to identify at-risk patients before they stopped coming. Retention efforts were reactive, not predictive.

What we set out to build

An interpretable model that flags high-risk patients early — giving staff time to intervene before the patient is already gone.

Why interpretability matters

Clinic leadership has no technical background. A 92% accurate black box is useless. We needed a model they could trust and act on.

Audience

Healthcare administrators and care coordinators — decision-makers who needed clear, actionable findings, not statistical outputs.

04 / 05 Key Findings

Two findings that reframed the retention strategy

FINDING_01
No-show rate dramatically increases churn risk
↑ churn risk
Patients with a high number of missed appointments are significantly more likely to disengage from Lackey Clinic. No-show rate was the strongest behavioral predictor in the model — more predictive than demographics or distance.
Intervention: automated SMS re-engagement trigger after 2nd no-show
FINDING_02
The Distance Paradox — proximity ≠ consistency
counterintuitive
Surprisingly, distance is not a significant predictor of churn. Distant patients may actually be less likely to churn — because they have fewer alternatives. The real risk lies with chronically ill and unemployed patients who silently drift away.
Implication: don't optimize for proximity. Optimize for vulnerability.
HIGHEST RETENTION COHORT

The healthy and employed. The highest-risk cohort: chronically ill and unemployed patients — who silently drift away without triggering obvious warning signals. These are the patients the model was designed to surface.

05 / 05 Recommendations

Three intervention strategies

// click each card to expand action details

📞
REC_01
Personalized Outreach
∨
Implement automated SMS re-engagement trigger immediately following a patient's second no-show
Personalized phone call or rescheduling support to reduce future disengagement
Flag high-risk patients in clinic management system for proactive coordinator outreach
Target: High no-show frequency patients
🏥
REC_02
Target Chronically Ill & Unemployed Patients
∨
Expand telehealth services to overcome transportation, time, and mobility barriers
Provide dedicated care coordinators for patients with chronic conditions
Connect unemployed patients with social service support and insurance navigation assistance
Target: Chronic illness + unemployment cohort
📍
REC_03
Improve Convenience for Nearby Patients
∨
Reduce wait times — nearby patients churn when friction outweighs convenience of proximity
Expand telehealth options even for local patients who prefer digital access
Introduce flexible scheduling windows to reduce scheduling conflicts for working patients
Target: Geographically proximate but low-engagement patients
↓
Estimated impact

Targeted proactive follow-up using model predictions — particularly the 48-hour re-engagement window after a missed appointment — is estimated to reduce patient churn by up to 45% in the highest-risk cohort.
